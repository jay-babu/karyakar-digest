Moving forward, reporting karyakars should begin to record sabha attendance in BKMS based on virtual post sabha goshti attendance.

2020 Virtual Post-Sabha - BK Karyakar Goshthis

A few select centers through North America conducted virtual sabha Goshthis directly following the national virtual kishore-kishori sabha the past two weeks. They were very well received because it allowed the kishores to discuss the sabha content to increase retention along with the ability to spend time together virtually.

Starting the weekend of 3/29 (this weekend) all centers should hold virtual post sabha Goshthis after the virtual Kishore sabha. Your respective mandal/vicharan Pujya Sant will try to attend as many virtual sabha Goshthis as possible. Your RC will help coordinate Pujya Sant presence/labh for your mandal.

Suggested planning tips:

  • Tag team with your RC to decide on what medium to be used for Virtual Sabha Goshthis (Zoom, etc)
  • Create a plan for informing your mandal kishores so they know this will be happening after the virtual sabha ends
  • Instructions on how to join the virtual sabha goshti (with relevant links) should be sent out to all the kishores and your RC by the Friday of every week.
  • For larger mandals, consider splitting the Goshthis into smaller sub-groups (example, K1/K2 gosthis can be separate)
2020 Virtual Post-Sabha - Goshthi Guidelines

Due to the current limitation on public gatherings, karyakar goshthis will continue virtually for each center. BK Pujya Santo will be attending their centers’ karyakar goshthis based on their availability. Local Coordinators should reach out to their BK Pujya Sant and Regional Coordinator (RC) to schedule a date/time that works for the Pujya Sant and the karyakar team. Local karyakar teams should still continue to take attendance during these goshthis and record the data on BKMS.

2020 Virtual BK Karyakar Goshthi - April

Yoganand: “Yog” means association. We have attained the yog of Bhagwan through the Satpurush. This yog is known as prapti and to experience happiness from this yog is known as praptino kef. Bhagwan Swaminarayan has explained in Vachanamrut Jetalpur 5 that “there is nothing greater than worshipping God.” We have attained Bhagwan himself on this Earth and there is nothing great now to achieve. Any misery we experience now is only a lack of our understanding. How can we tackle any obstacles that stop us from constantly experiencing praptino kef and how can we keep this prapti in mind at all times to truly feel the highest for of happiness?

April Karyakar Goshthi
