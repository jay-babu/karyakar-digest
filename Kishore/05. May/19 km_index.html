2020 Virtual Karyakar Goshthi: Divyanand
Divyanand: Mahant Swami Maharaj references the following quote from Purushottam Bolya Prite often: “Bhagwanne bhajta sarve harijan divya chhe. A marm je koi samje, dhare, vichare, te magan thai jay chhe, jag jiti jay chhe ane athe pahor teno anand talto nathi.”
Understanding all to be divya is the only means to experiencing the highest form of happiness. How can we constantly maintain divyabhav in Bhagwan, the Sant, their niyams, the sanstha as well as in all santo-haribhakto? If we can master this skill, nothing is left to perform to experience constant happiness forever.

PARAM ANAND MOMENT

“The wish of Maharaj and Bapa prevails. So, one should remain happy.”
On 25 January 1974, Pramukh Swami Maharaj departed with nine sadhus amid a joyous farewell for his first Satsang tour abroad since becoming our Guru in 1971. The plane took off from Mumbai at 10:15 am and landed in Nairobi at 1:22 pm. All the passengers disembarked but Pramukh Swami Maharaj and the sadhus were ordered to stay aboard. Then at 4:15 pm, without any reason, they were instructed to fly back to Mumbai. Everyone in the group was saddened by the unexpected order to return.
When Bhaktipriya Swami (Kothari Swami) gave his diary to Swamishri during their flight back to Mumbai, he wrote, "The wish of Maharaj and Bapa (Yogiji Maharaj) prevails. So, one should remain happy. Accept the ways they desire for you. Do not be pained or become miserable. Become Aksharrup and offer devotion to God, then one will not be miserable." At 2:45 am Swamishri arrived in Mumbai. After reaching the Swaminarayan Mandir he prostrated to Thakorji, Swamishri asked for His forgiveness in not being able to offer Him food during their return journey to Mumbai.
Swamishri then had a fresh meal prepared at night and offered it to Thakorji while singing thal. Swamishri was not concerned about the insult and the disrespectful manner of being sent back, but rather concerned about Thakorji who had not been offered any food. The next day Swamishri discoursed in a happy manner, inspiring laughter and joy in all the sadhus and devotees, thus making them forget the misery of him being sent back.
Despite facing adverse situations, Swamishri consistently adhered to maintaining an aura of peace around him. Not once during this time of adversity did he become bitter, irritated, or question the circumstances of what had occurred. Understanding that this was all due to Maharaj’s wish, he remained unphased by this otherwise dreadful situation.

During times of difficulty, I find myself thinking ill of others, or blaming circumstances or other factors/people for the situation I am in. However, it’s most important that in these moments, I force a pause on all these thoughts clouding my judgment and clear my mind. I can recall many times in my life either in school, work, or seva where I let the smallest of irritants disturb my mood causing me to feel distressed throughout the day.

Somedays, I might not get my morning coffee, or get thrown a last-minute assignment at school or work, or get told that a seva task I had already completed needs to be re- done. Reading this now, you may think these moments of inconvenience seem trivial. But, in the moment, these small disturbances and others can create a fury of mental anguish.

However, after a grand send-off preparing Swamishri to embark on his first satsang tour outside of India, he came back without placing a single foot off the plane. Through this normally humiliating ordeal, Swamishri remained ever blissful, understanding Maharaj and Swami to be Sarva-Karta (all-doer). The next time we feel like our day cannot get any worse, let’s remember this prasang of Pramukh Swami Maharaj and his Sarva- Karta no vichar.
