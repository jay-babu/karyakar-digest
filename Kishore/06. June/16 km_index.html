Gadhadā I - 16

“

A wise devotee of God who can discern between sat and asat identifies the flaws within himself and thoughtfully eradicates them. He also rejects any apparent flaws he perceives in the Sant or a satsangi and imbibes only their virtues. Moreover, he never perceives any faults whatsoever in God.



”

COVID-19 Impacted Pravrutti
Each month we have been assessing what is feasible and releasing updates for the next couple of months of BK Pravrutti. We know everyone is anxious about the future and has lots of questions about how we plan to adjust our Pravrutti as COVID-19 continues to impact us.
As such, we want to address key karyakar-facing events that we had coming up this year. Additionally, we will release a comprehensive guide (LGOTSP for COVID-19 Impacted Pravrutti) in next week’s digest. For now, please take note of the following:
    Regional KarCon - RKC (October)
    • Due to the impact of COVID-19, we want to be financially responsible at this time. Given that RKC has a large financial impact on both the Sanstha and Karyakars through grants and registration fees, respectively, and the complications of traveling in the current climate, RKC will be conducted virtually.
    • Common Sessions (Values and Main Messages) and Team Development Sessions will be scheduled in October. For Common Sessions, karyakars will be provided with a webcast link to join on October 17th and 24th. Thereafter, karyakars will split into small goshthi groups to discuss the values. For Team Breakouts, karyakars will join their seva-specific track sessions between October 17th and November 8th, as determined by the Regional team. More details to follow.
    • Center Analysis (November-December) will be scheduled separately by each center.
    Bhakti Talks from RKC (July-August)
    • Bhakti Talks, originally part of RKC, are virtual workshop-like sessions that provide a ‘choose your path’ type of learning. Karyakars will be able to sign-up for topics which interest them, and which are presented by subject-matter experts.
    • Please take note of the following timelines, as you may see registration and other requests come through in the weeks ahead.
      • Registration will take place any time between now and June 30th.
      • Talks will be scheduled to occur any time between July 15th and August 15th.
      • By July 15th, karyakars will receive the timings and video call links for the sessions they selected.

    PARAM ANAND MOMENT

    "I am atma and Paramatma is eternally within me."
    Realizing oneself as atma, which is not born and does not die, and offering devotion to Paramatma creates an exalted spiritual state that is way beyond the common, momentary experiences of meditation, visions, and well-being. Through satsang and spiritual endeavors, people rise above worldly desires and fears! Even beyond the fear of death. Bhagwanji Mandavia was a dynamic business administrator in Toronto. Active in the Indo-Canada Federation, and in guiding the Gujarati community, Bhagwanji was enjoying success at its best. Then in 1985, he was diagnosed with leukemia.
    Not disclosing it to anyone except a close friend or two, he continued with his life. All throughout, his satsang with Swamishri had strengthened him beyond belief. By 1988, he was hospitalized for a bone-marrow transplant. All the pains and pressures of loneliness, illness, depression, and death were upon him. Still, his smile never lost its luster nor did his voice lose its vibrancy. No wonder, while he phoned his friends, almost dying on his bed, they still thought Bhagwanji was in office! His inner strength and stability are spelled in the reply he wrote before his death to his brother in India who had expressed great grief about his terminal illness!
    “Physical pain comes to one and all. So one must never worry about disease and death. One must never leave the refuge of a saint like Pramukh Swami Maharaj in whom God resides. Our faith in God and Swamiji should remain unshakeable.”

    Undeterred by his ambiance full of illness and death, Bhagwanji Mandavia maintained his normal life and his connection with Swamishri. Rather than lingering with worry and regret, he took every moment to ensure that his bond with Swamishri remained unshakeable. He remained grounded to his values, while his world began spiraling out of control all while his level of faith and perseverance only continued to flourish.

    There may be times in life, where I may find myself distancing from my guru due to hardships. However, it’s in those moments that I should recall Bhagwanji Mandavia’s fierce determination to keep Swamishri at heart. Despite Bhagwanji Mandavia’s illness, he made sure to keep his relationship with Swamishri a priority. In the same way, when I sense my connection weakening, I will make a genuine effort to keep myself affixed to Swamishri. By engaging in any opportunity of bhakti that comes my way, I will be sure to secure my tether to Swamishri like an iron nail bound to an iron board.

    At the end of the day, Swamishri’s virtues have shone onto many people, alleviating their challenges and fears. He has gone through the lengthy effort to strengthen our bond, likewise, I should take time out of my day to do the same. It may translate into doing five more malas in my puja, making sure I do chesta, or do satsang reading, but no matter what, I will make sure to enhance my connection with my guru.
