Mahant Swami Maharaj's Historic Gift to Us
On the auspicious occasion of Guru Purnima, Guruhari Param Pujya Mahant Swami Maharaj presented a special gift to the BAPS satsang community in the form of a book.
Written by Pragat Brahmaswarup Mahant Swami Maharaj himself in Gujarati, ‘Satsang Diksha’ is a concise text which forms a part of the voluminous ‘Akshar-Purushottam Samhita’. The Gujarati prose is also presented as 315 Sanskrit verses.
In ‘Satsang Diksha’, based on the teachings of Bhagwan Swaminarayan, Mahant Swami Maharaj concisely describes the philosophical principles to be understood and the daily personal practices of devotion to be undertaken by all BAPS devotees.
Mahant Swami Maharaj has given āgnā to sing the āhvān mantra and punarāgaman mantra in one's daily puja according to the Vedic-style. A video of how to sing it in the Vedic-style is included with this message along with a card and phone wallpapers that have the puja shloks written on them. We should all sing the mantra this way.
Material in English, Gujarati and Hindi is available.

PARAM ANAND MOMENT

“Your love for me is the same as before.”
An ordinary shepherd of Sarangpur, Raghu Bharwad, came for Pramukh Swami Maharaj’s darshan. Swamishri called him lovingly, “Come Raghuvir!” Raghu, took off his pagh and bowed his head before Swamishri and said, “Jay Swaminarayan.” “Where have you been all these days? Why didn’t you come to see me earlier? Swamishri enquired. “I have been sick for the past three to four days. See, here are the tablets…” and so saying, Raghu showed them to Swamishri. Swamishri listened to him with great interest.
“His children have built proper homes by the main road,” a sadhu informed Swamishri. “Once, I’d like to take Swamishri there to sanctify it,” Raghu spoke in a rustic, candid tone. “These sadhus will come,” Swamishri replied. Raghu was happy at the proposal and responded, “Your love for me is the same as before.” “Come to the mandir daily for Thakorji’s darshan. This is a pilgrim place,” Swamishri told Raghu, “in which Ganga, Haridwar, Rishikesh, Dwarka, and all pilgrim places are included.”
“Bless me with your grace always. It means everything to me,” Raghu asked while he was about to leave. Swamishri raised his hand and blessed him. Though an ordinary shepherd, Swamishri’s rapport and interest in Raghu reflect his care and love for ordinary rural devotees like him.

Whether Pramukh Swami Maharaj is advising the utmost prestigious scholar in Mumbai or mentoring the most meager dalit in Padra, he has always shown affection to his beloved devotees, regardless of status. By constantly showering boundless hope and guidance upon poor and wealthy alike, Swamishri has guided countless over their tallest walls of hardship and towards the brightest forms of sukh.

With the annual summer's wave of laziness and relaxation slowly starting to engulf us, our sleep cycles slowly inching toward becoming nocturnal, and our niyams getting pushed away by other activities, it’s becoming increasingly more important to continue imbibing the messages we learn from our gurus. By taking one step into Swamishri’s life we can immediately see that he has treated every single person he has met with the highest level of care and love, despite how they may look superficially. If we sincerely wish to influence change we must mimic the actions of our gurus. By shifting our habits to constantly view the good qualities within others, we can slowly start to imbibe this quality within our own lives; only then will we be able to clearly see the divinity within others, just as our gurus have.

Furthermore, Swamishri took time out of his busy schedule and listened with genuine care and concern, by taking this lesson to heart I can pay it forward through comforting my peers during their troubling moments. Additionally, Swamishri’s love has pierced through barriers of class, creed, and wealth. In the same manner, I will make it my utmost priority to make my mandir a welcoming environment by not judging fellow haribhakto based on superficial characteristics. Through taking these steps, I will instill the same values that Swamishri has shown and see the divinity in those around me.
