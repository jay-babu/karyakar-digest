PARAM ANAND MOMENT

An end to 200 years of conflict.
The villages of Kukad and Odarka in the district of Bhavnagar were notorious for a conflict that had protracted for 200 years. For generations the Rajput citizens of both villages were hostile to each other and fought bitterly over a piece of disputed pastoral land. Many had died because of the issue. And the volcano of hatred was still active, sometimes spitting its deathly lava to claim a few more lives. Year after year their hatred escalated eventually reaching the point where the two villages abstained from drinking the water from each other’s village. Through the decades, many arbitrators like the Maharaja of Bhavnagar, Krishnakumarsinhji, as well as British and Indian government officials have tried to resolve the conflict, but they failed. After countless exhausted attempts, no one believed the conflict between Kukad and Odarka would end.
Yet remarkably, the deadlocked situation took a new turn when the transformation of Ramsangh Bapu, a hard-core criminal who wrecked terror in the region of Ghogha, took place through his association with Swamishri. Being a resident of Odarka, he expressed his remorse for his sins. He appealed to Swamishri to help resolve the hostility between Odarka and Kukad. On April 12th 1990, Swamishri visited Odarka and went to the disputed land on its outskirts. Here, Swamishri ceremoniously bathed the tombstones of those who had been killed in the conflict with panchamrut, and water mixed from both villages. Through combining the water of both villages, Swamishi’s had allowed them to overcome their bickering, signifying the glorious resolution between Kukad and Odarka. Prayers were offered for the redemption of all those who had died in the conflict and for an end to the pledge of not drinking water from each other’s village. Then arti was performed in the presence of senior members of the Kshatriya community of Saurashtra.
The occasion climaxed when the Darbars drank water from each other’s village offered by Swamishri. Two hundred years of vendetta was dissolved with the blessings of Swamishri. Finally, Swamishri addressed the gathering, “I am very happy today because you have given up your hatred for each other. You have resolved it with an open and compassionate heart. Bhagwan Swaminarayan will bless your villages. To give up past hatred is a sign of nobility. Progress is only possible if one forgets hate. You have pledged to drink water from each other’s village, but there is one thing you have to abstain from, and that is drinking liquor. “The mission of God and His holy Sadhu is to bring people closer to each other. I pray that you attain the true purpose of human birth – liberation, through devotion to God and by helping and serving others.” This historic event of extinguishing the fires of hatred and inspiring springs of love and friendship will forever be etched in the annals of history. Swamishri was extremely happy for the restoration of peace in the hearts of the people of Odarka and Kukad.

There have been countless prasangs of our gurus showing boundless love to those seeking to leave behind their swabhavs. From dealing with a middle-aged man furiously insulting him within his own uttaro to ravenous criminals who cultured fear in thousands just by the sound of their name, Pramukh Swami Maharaj has effortlessly excused the sins of whomever renounced their flaws. By spreading the values of unity and tolerance, Swamishri has shown what it means to forgive and forget. Seeing Maharaj within each individual, Swamishri has highlighted the importance of recognizing that divinity and therefore treating each other with love, kindness, and respect.

Time and time again, I encounter scenarios where the lessons our gurus have taught us are tested. Whether someone is insulting me on social media, judging the way I look, or mocking something I care about, being able to control my emotions and learn to forgive instantly will help me feel shanti and allow me to live blissfully. I will pay forward all of Swamishri’s efforts by learning to keep Samp even when others might not be inclined to in return. By taking this difficult step, Swamishri has been able to transform the most concentrated forms of hatred into the purest forms of love. Similarly, I desire to identify the flaws within my own character and replenish them with acts of love and forgiveness. In doing so, not only will I create a stronger relationship with those around me, but also further strengthen my jodaan with Swamishri.
