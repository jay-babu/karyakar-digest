PARAM ANAND MOMENT

Experiencing Joy In Every Moment
On July 7th, 1980, Pramukh Swami Maharaj was on a nagaryatra moving atop a small elephant, giving everyone his darshan and labh. The procession was a four mile journey from Alperton High School sports ground to Meadow Garth, Neasden, North West London, where he consecrated the site for where the largest Hindu temple outside of India would be built.
During the trip, both Pramukh Swami Maharaj and Pujya Atmaswarup Swami were seated on top of the elephant, where there was limited space for both of them to occupy. In conjunction with the lengthy procession, the summer sun showered its scorching heat overtop Swamishri and the devotees who eagerly waited for darshan. Pujya Atmaswarup Swami became irritated with the tiresome situation however when he glanced over at Swamishri, he saw nothing but a joyous smile on his face sustained from having the darshan of all the devotees in the procession.
Even with all of these unfavorable factors, Swamishri disregarded the physical difficulties and continued to maintain a delightful demeanor during the entirety of the nagaryatra, gaining stamina from his beloved haribhakto whose accompaniment brought him utmost joy.

No matter if it be minor discomfort experienced through frequent illness or the dire symptoms of a heart attack, throughout Pramukh Swami Maharaj’s life not once has he let his external dukh (pain) interfere and restrain him from experiencing his internal, everlasting joy from pleasing his Guru and connecting with Pujya Santo and fellow haribhakto. His degree of compassion for his devotees reaches past any physical barrier his body or environment may present.

Taking a deeper glance, Swamishri’s mindset reflects the inverse of how we behave: suppressing his own needs and embracing physical discomfort, to devote his time, mind, body, and energy to tend to the needs of others. Considering the circumstances of others and their wishes before acknowledging his own concerns, highlights Swamishri’s altruism and his embodiment of being a Das na Das (a servant of a servant).

Swamishri exemplifies the value of selflessness and has lived his life according to his motto “In the joy of others, lies our own”. Similarly, I too can engage in selfless service and experience that same sukh and anand Swamishri is engrossed in at all times. My seva provides me with the perfect opportunity to achieve this and so I pray that I can continue doing my seva wholeheartedly, imbibe the quality of Das na Das in my own life, and earn Maharaj and Swami’s ultimate blessings and raajipo.
